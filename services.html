Cleft Lip Surgery

Repair of a split or opening in the upper lip. Usually performed when a baby is 3–6 months old. The surgeon stitches the lip tissue together and reshapes the lip and nose area.

Cleft Palate Surgery

Repair of a gap in the roof of the mouth (palate). Done to improve speech, feeding, and swallowing. The soft and hard palate tissues are reconstructed and closed.

Cleft Lip and Palate Combined Surgery

For children with both defects extending from the lip through the gums and palate. Treatment involves multiple reconstructive procedures combining lip and palate repair.

Craniofacial Surgery

Correction of craniofacial abnormalities (structural deformities of the skull and face). Examples include congenital facial bone malformations
